Indonesian Cuisine .

Collection of various regional culinary traditions that formed the archipelagic nation of Indonesia. Indonesia has around 5,350 traditional recipes.

History of Indonesian Food

Due to its beneficial geographic location, Indonesia has attracted many traders, pirates and adventurers from all over the world. Our archipelago was most known for rich resources of spices, especially in the eastern region, and these spice-hunters soon turned into immigrants and permanent dwellers.

Not only known as spice producers, Indonesia also welcomed a wide array of exotic foods from around the globe. Most of these latest-recipes were brought in by traders and Hindu missionaries from India, Europe to the Middle East. As such, Indonesia has a great global influence, even right from the start.

Most Popular Recipes

BALI

Sate Lilit

Sate lilit is one of the culinary delights that we can find in Bali. Sate lilit is satay made by wrapping raw materials on a skewer. The raw materials for making satay wraps are usually fish, pork, beef, or chicken.

MANADO

Bubur Manado

Tinutuan or Manado Porridge is a mixture of various kinds of vegetables, does not contain meat, so this food can be a social food between community groups in Manado.

PALEMBANG

Pempek

A food made from soft ground fish meat mixed with starch or sago flour, as well as a composition of several other ingredients such as eggs, mashed garlic, flavoring, and salt. Pempek is usually served with vinegar sauce which has a sour, sweet, and spicy taste.

BANDUNG

Seblak

Seblak is an Sundanese savoury and spicy dish, originating from the Sundanese region in West Java, Indonesia. Made of wet krupuk (traditional Indonesian crackers) cooked with protein sources (egg, chicken, seafood or beef) in spicy sauce.
